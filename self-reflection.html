Today's reflection
Finding clarity through constraints

Today's design sprint taught me something unexpected — tight constraints don't limit creativity, they focus it. We had two hours to redesign the onboarding flow, a problem I'd been overthinking for weeks. The deadline forced me to trust my instincts.

I tend to treat open-ended time as a gift, but today proved that open-ended can become an excuse. When the clock was running, I made decisions. Clear, committed decisions. And most of them were right.

Guided Prompt
What did you do today that required courage, even in a small way?
I pushed back on a stakeholder's request to add five more options to the navigation. I said no — politely, with reasoning — and held the line. Six months ago I would have quietly said yes and added them. Small moment, but it felt significant.
